01 · Logging In
How to access the system securely

When you open the ASO System, you'll see a login screen. Each user has their own username and password set by the administrator.

1
Enter your username
Type your assigned username in the Username field. The default admin account uses admin.
2
Enter your password
Type your password. You can change passwords anytime in Settings → Password Manager.
3
Click "Sign In →"
You'll be taken directly to the Dashboard. If credentials are incorrect, a red error message will appear — double-check your username and try again.
⚠️
Change the default password immediately
Go to Settings → Password Manager and update the admin password before using the system in production.

03 · Timesheet — Adding Shifts
Recording work shifts and viewing hours

The Timesheet page is where all shift data lives. You add new shifts here, and the system automatically calculates regular vs. overtime hours based on your configured thresholds.

How to Add a Shift
1
Select the Date — Click the Date field and pick the date the shift occurred.
2
Choose a Staff Member — Click the Staff dropdown and select the employee who worked the shift.
3
Select a Location — Pick the work site from the Location dropdown. Locations are configured in Settings.
4
Set Start & End Times — Type any start and end time (e.g. 10:26). The system previews calculated hours in real time.
5
Click "+ Add Shift" — The shift is saved and appears in the table below. A green toast will confirm the addition.
⏰
Shifts over 24 hours
If a shift spans more than 24 hours on a given day, the system will automatically flag it and ask for a reason before saving. The shift will go into the Approvals queue.
🚫
Overlapping shifts are blocked
The system won't let you save a shift whose time range overlaps with an existing shift for the same person on the same day — including overnight shifts that cross midnight. You'll get a clear error naming the conflicting shift so you can fix the times or edit the existing entry instead.

04 · Shift Approvals
Reviewing and authorizing exceptional shifts

The Approvals page handles shifts where an employee's combined daily total exceeds 24 hours. These require a supervisor to review the stated reason and either approve or deny the entry before it counts toward payroll.

🔴
Why this matters
Shifts over 24 hours on a single day are unusual and may indicate a data-entry error. This approval step prevents accidental overtime inflation and ensures a supervisor has reviewed every long shift.
Approval Workflow
1
Shift is submitted with a reason
When logging a shift that would push the daily total over 24 hours, the system prompts for a reason before saving. That reason is stored and shown on the Approvals page.
2
Supervisor reviews the reason
Go to Approvals in the sidebar. Pending shifts appear with the employee name, date, total hours, and stated reason.
3
Click Approve or Deny
✅ Approve moves the shift to the approved list and includes it in payroll calculations. ✕ Deny removes the shift from the system entirely.
🔴
Approval count badge: A red badge appears on the Approvals sidebar link whenever there are pending items — so you'll never miss one. This badge now also counts pending time-off requests, not just shift exceptions.
Time Off Requests

Below the shift approvals, this same page has a "Time Off Requests" table showing every PTO request from employee self-service accounts — pending, approved, and denied. Each pending request shows the employee, type (vacation/sick/personal), dates, hours, and any note they included. Click ✅ Approve or ✕ Deny. Approving deducts the hours from that person's PTO balance immediately; denying leaves their balance untouched.

10 · Tips & Troubleshooting
Common questions and quick fixes
⚠️ Staff not appearing in Timesheet dropdown
Check that the employee's status is set to Active in the Staff page. Inactive employees are hidden from dropdowns.
⚠️ Wrong overtime amount shown
Verify the OT threshold in Settings matches your policy. Also confirm the staff member's hourly rate is correct in the Staff Register.
⚠️ Accidentally added wrong shift
Find the shift in the Timesheet table and click the delete button on that row. There is no undo, so double-check before deleting.
⚠️ Report shows no data
Check your filters — the date range or pay period may not include any shifts. Try "All Time" as a quick test. Also confirm at least one staff chip is selected.
⚠️ Forgot admin password
Since the system runs locally in a browser, you can open the browser's developer console and reset the data, or contact your system administrator.
⚠️ Data not saving between sessions
The ASO System stores data in your browser's local storage. Do not clear browser data or switch browsers — this will erase all records. Use the same browser on the same device each time.
Color & Badge Guide
Blue — General information, active selections, and totals
Green — Regular (non-OT) shifts, approved items, success
Amber — Overtime hours and warnings requiring attention
Red — Overtime pay costs and items requiring urgent action

The Locations page (under Management in the sidebar) lets you define, rename, and remove the work sites that appear in the Timesheet and Report Builder. Each location can also carry its own billing rate used for cost reporting.

1

12 · Data Manager
Backup, restore, and reset the database

The Data Manager (sidebar → Setup) is your hub for keeping data safe. Your data lives in a real database on the server, not in your browser — but exporting a manual backup periodically, and storing it somewhere other than the server, is still strongly recommended in case of accidental data changes.

🗃️
Automatic server backups
The server automatically snapshots the whole database once a day (the last 14 are kept), and you can trigger one manually anytime with the "Backup Now" button on this page. These live on the same server as your live data, so they protect against accidental mistakes (a bad import, an accidental bulk change) — but not against the server itself failing. Keep exporting the JSON backup below periodically and store a copy somewhere separate, like your own computer or a cloud drive.
Exporting a Backup
1
Click ⬇️ Export Backup — Downloads a .json file named with today's date (e.g., ASO_backup_2025-06-12.json).
2
Save the file in a safe place — Store it on a network drive, USB stick, or cloud folder. Do not rely solely on the browser.
Restoring from Backup
1
Click ⬆️ Import Backup — A file picker opens. Select your .json backup file.
2
Confirm the import — A dialog will show the backup date and ask you to confirm. Importing replaces all current data, so export a fresh backup first if needed.
💡
What's included in a backup
All shifts · Staff roster (incl. PTO balances) · Locations & rates · Users & passwords · Pay period config & tax/benefit defaults · Payroll history & pay stubs · Time off requests · Approved exceptions · Date correction log · Audit trail
⛔
Factory Reset — use with extreme caution
The Reset to Factory Defaults button permanently deletes all data — shifts, staff, config, and users. This cannot be undone. Always export a backup before using this option.

16 · Payroll
Running payroll for a pay period, start to finish

Payroll (sidebar → Main) takes your recorded shifts for a period and turns them into gross pay, net pay, and printable pay stubs — in two steps.

Step 1 — Review & Confirm
1
Pick a Pay Period — The dropdown lists any period with recorded shifts, current or past.
2
Check the hours & gross pay table — Broken down by house/location, so you can catch a missing or wrong shift before running numbers.
3
Taxes & benefits are already filled in — Pulled automatically from whatever you saved on the Taxes & Benefits page. You can tweak anything for just this one period without touching your saved defaults.
4
Set the Pay Date, click Confirm & Run Payroll — This locks the period's timesheets (no more edits without unlocking) and calculates everything in one step.
Step 2 — Payroll Complete

Shows a Net Pay Summary per employee (gross, each tax/benefit deducted, net), plus the Pay Stubs panel below — pick anyone from the dropdown, preview their stub, and print one or all of them. Export CSV downloads both the hours-by-house detail and the net-pay summary in one file.

⚠️
Made a mistake?
Click "🔓 Unlock & Recalculate" to undo the confirm/finalize and go back to Step 1 with your timesheets editable again.

17 · Taxes & Benefits
Set your standard withholdings and benefits once

This page (sidebar → Setup) is where you configure the list of taxes and benefits that get applied automatically every time you run payroll — so you don't rebuild the same list every pay period.

1
Add a tax or benefit — Give it any name (Federal Withholding, Health Insurance, 401(k) — anything).
2
Choose % of Gross or Flat $ Amount — Percentage deductions scale with each paycheck; flat amounts stay the same every time (e.g. a fixed insurance premium).
3
Click Save as Default — Every new pay period's Payroll page will now start with this exact list already filled in.
💡
These are estimates, not IRS tax tables. Percentage-based withholding here is a flat rate you set, not real federal/state bracket calculations tied to W-4 elections. Treat Payroll's numbers as a solid working estimate and pay-stub generator — for anything filed with tax authorities, have an accountant or your payroll processor verify the real withholding.

18 · My Schedule & Pay (Employee Portal)
What staff see when they log in with an Employee account

Anyone logging in with an Employee role account sees a stripped-down sidebar with just one item — "My Schedule & Pay" — and nothing else. This page has three parts:

📅
My Shifts
Their own shift history and this period's hours — nobody else's.
🧾
My Pay Stubs
A dropdown of their own finalized pay periods, with a full printable stub for each.
🌴
Time Off
Their current PTO balance, a request form (type, dates, hours, optional note), and their own request history with status.
🔒
This is enforced by the server, not just hidden in the browser
An employee account only ever receives their own data from the server in the first place — it's not that other people's data is hidden on screen, it's that it never gets sent to their browser at all. Submitting a time-off request always attaches to their own record, even if someone tried to tamper with the request.

💡
This is especially useful for employee self-service accounts — staff can check their schedule, pull up a pay stub, or request time off from their own phone without needing a desktop.
